Recommendations for diet:

Macronutrients: Make sure to include all three macronutrients - carbohydrates, proteins, and fats - in your meals.

Carbohydrates: Choose complex carbohydrates like whole grains (e.g., brown rice, quinoa, whole wheat bread) and starchy vegetables (e.g., sweet potatoes, corn, peas) for sustained energy.

Proteins: Include lean sources of protein such as skinless poultry, fish, lean meats, legumes, tofu, or Greek yogurt to support muscle growth and repair.

Fats: Include healthy fats from sources like avocados, nuts, seeds, olive oil, and fatty fish (e.g., salmon, mackerel) for essential fatty acids and fat-soluble vitamins.

Fruits and vegetables: Incorporate a variety of fruits and vegetables to ensure an adequate intake of vitamins, minerals, and fiber. Aim for at least 5 servings of fruits and vegetables per day.

Whole foods: Focus on whole, unprocessed foods whenever possible, as they tend to be more nutrient-dense. Avoid or limit processed foods high in added sugars, unhealthy fats, and sodium.

Portion sizes: Pay attention to portion sizes to ensure you are consuming an appropriate amount of food to meet your calorie goals. Using measuring cups, a food scale, or nutrition tracking apps can help you estimate portion sizes accurately.

Hydration: Drink plenty of water throughout the day to stay hydrated. Limit sugary beverages and opt for water, herbal tea, or unsweetened beverages.

Meal planning: Plan your meals in advance to ensure a well-balanced and calorie-appropriate diet. Include a mix of food groups in each meal and consider meal prepping to make healthy eating more convenient.

Individualize your needs: These guidelines are general recommendations, but everyone's nutritional needs and preferences are unique. It's important to consult with a registered dietitian or healthcare professional who can provide personalized advice based on your specific goals, dietary restrictions, and medical conditions.
